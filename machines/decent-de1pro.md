- **Heating:** No traditional boiler; 3-phase heating element with integrated pressure transducer
- **Pump:** Integrated solenoid pump, software-controlled flow precision
- **Group:** Proprietary DE1 design with integrated pressure transducer
- **Reservoir:** 2 L glazed porcelain
- **Voltage:** 110 V and 220 V variants
- **Build:** All-metal chassis (brushed steel front with mirrored lip), glazed porcelain tank and drip tray; assembled in Hong Kong

## Key features

The Decent rethinks the espresso machine from first principles. There are no boilers — the heating system uses three-phase flash heaters that can ramp brew temperature on demand and supply steam via a different control algorithm. A 7" Android-powered touchscreen runs custom firmware.

What this enables:

- **Real-time pressure profiling** — design or download any pressure-vs-time curve (Slayer, Londinium lever, custom), execute repeatedly to 0.1 bar precision
- **Real-time flow profiling** — directly control flow rate rather than working against it
- **Real-time temperature profiling** — vary brew temperature during the shot (harder to do on any boiler machine)
- **Shot data logging** — every shot recorded with pressure/flow/temperature curves, weight if scale integrated
- **Community profile sharing** via [Visualizer.coffee](https://visualizer.coffee) — 8,400+ public profiles
- **Firmware upgradeable** — Decent ships meaningful feature updates 2-4 times per year; your machine improves over time
- **Rated for 200,000+ shots** (the DE1Pro's commercial-grade heater lifetime claim)

Compared to the Bianca: the Bianca's paddle gives you mechanical flow control. The Decent gives you software-defined pressure, flow, AND temperature control, with data logging and community profile sharing. It is a different category of machine.

## Steam and milk workflow

The Decent's steam system is not traditional. Flash heating provides steam on demand; it's strong enough for standard home milk drinks and quickly recovers. The wand is a proprietary no-burn design.

For café-speed multi-pitcher work, a traditional dual boiler with a 1-2 L dedicated steam boiler is still better. For one to four drinks per session, the Decent is competent.

This is the Decent's weakest area relative to traditional machines. Decent knows this and has continued to refine firmware for better steam consistency.

## Brew workflow and temperature stability

The Decent's brew workflow is different:

- Pick a profile from the onboard library or Visualizer
- Pull shot — the machine executes the profile automatically
- See real-time graph of pressure, flow, temperature on the touchscreen
- Iterate by comparing shot-to-shot charts

Temperature precision is industry-leading (flash heating + closed-loop control). Profiling is genuinely differentiated — you can pull a shot that would be impossible on any mechanical machine.

Learning curve is steep. Decent's own documentation notes that new users typically need 20-30 shots to produce good espresso; 3-4 weeks to be comfortable with the interface. This is the opposite of the Bambino Plus's day-one accessibility.

## Grinder pairing

Specialita is fine for most profiles. For serious profiling and light-roast pushing, a higher-end single-dose grinder (Niche Zero, Weber EG1, Lagom P64) would unlock more ceiling. But the Decent works with any good grinder; there's no fundamental mismatch with the Specialita.

## Complexity and learning curve

Highest on this list. You're not just learning espresso — you're learning a software interface, shot profile design, and an ecosystem of shared profiles. Decent users describe it as "a hobby within a hobby."

If that sounds rewarding, the Decent is exceptional. If it sounds exhausting, buy a Bianca or Synchronika instead.

## Modification and upgrade potential

Negligible hardware mods; the machine is closed-box. But the modification layer is software:

- **Profile sharing on Visualizer** — download any community profile, compare your shot charts to the original
- **Beanconqueror** integration (espresso tracking apps)
- **Scale integration** (Acaia Pearl, Bluetooth-compatible scales trigger automated shot cutoff by weight)
- **Firmware updates** — Decent ships new features (pre-infusion modes, temperature algorithms, UI improvements) regularly

"Upgrade your machine" on a Decent means install new firmware. Different paradigm.

## Pros and cons

**Pros**
- **Only machine on this list with software-defined profiling** at pressure, flow, AND temperature levels
- Shot-by-shot data logging with Visualizer integration (8,400+ community profiles)
- **<5 min warmup**, fastest on this list
- Compact (9 in wide, 30 lb); smallest footprint of any DB-class machine here
- Firmware upgradeable — your machine gets better over time
- Plumb-in options include mains pressure, non-pressurized tank, and drain kit
- Rated for 200,000+ shots; commercial-grade heater lifetime
- Direct manufacturer sales and support

**Cons**
- **Steepest learning curve** on this list (3-4 weeks typical)
- **Steam power weakest** of the DB tier (flash heater vs dedicated steam boiler)
- $3,699 direct only; no third-party retailers, no sale pricing
- Software-dependent — troubleshooting requires firmware understanding
- No mechanical fallback; if the software has a bug, you wait for a patch
- Niche ecosystem — fewer "traditional" accessories, less overlap with community outside Decent
- Requires occasional internet connection for firmware updates and profile sync
- DE1XL ($3,999) and DE1XXL ($4,399) blur the value proposition; pick carefully

## Who it's for

The engineer, the scientist, the experimenter. Someone who finds joy in data, profile curves, and optimization loops. Also: someone who drinks light and medium-light roasts, where pressure/flow profiling is most valuable.

**Not** for you if you want to just make espresso and enjoy it without thinking. Not for you if milk drinks are the primary use case (steam is adequate, not exceptional). Not for you if you want third-party retailer relationships or traditional-machine community.

For an even milk/espresso user with $3,700 budget: the Decent is the right buy **if and only if** you're energized by the idea of learning a new interface and iterating on shot profiles. The espresso side will be exceptional; the milk side will be adequate. If adequate isn't enough, the Bianca at $3,000 is a better milk machine (and still has paddle flow control for most of the profiling fun).
