## Specs

- **Brew boiler:** 0.4 L (insulated, improved vs Pro 300)
- **Steam boiler:** 0.75 L (insulated, improved vs Pro 300)
- **Pump:** Ulka vibratory (52 W)
- **Group:** Chrome-plated saturated ring group with mechanical pre-infusion
- **Reservoir:** 2.8 L
- **Wattage:** 1600 W (US), 2200 W (EU)
- **Voltage:** 110-120 V confirmed
- **Build:** Stainless steel chassis, black powder-coated housing with brushed front plate; walnut accents available

## Key features

The Move inherits the Pro 300's compact saturated-group architecture and adds modernization that matches the Ride's interface:

- **OLED PID display** with per-degree brew and steam temperature control
- **Dual independent PID** — each boiler tuned separately
- **Integrated shot timer** (new to this tier; Pro 300 didn't have one standard)
- **Programmable volumetrics** — two custom shot buttons
- **Scheduled on/off** — program the machine to warm up before you wake
- **Modular portafilter** with silicone spout attachments
- **Improved steam power** vs the Pro 300 (Whole Latte Love: ~25-27 seconds for 5 oz milk)

What the saturated group buys you: excellent brew temperature stability (the group is the brew boiler's extension), passive pre-infusion without E61 mechanical complexity, and simpler internals. What it costs you: no compatibility with E61 flow control kits. Profitec has not announced a saturated-group flow control solution as of April 2026.

## Steam and milk workflow

0.75 L steam boiler is small for a DB but adequate for single-session home drink volumes. The no-burn 2-hole wand is the same as the rest of the modern Profitec line. Milk recovery is slower than full-size DBs (Pro 600/Ride have 1.0 L; Synchronika has 2.0 L), but fine for 1-2 drinks.

Simultaneous brew and steam, as expected.

## Brew workflow and temperature stability

Saturated group + dual PID = excellent thermal stability. Reviewer reports put shot-to-shot variance at ±2 °F (~±1 °C). No E61 thermosiphon to manage; pull the shot, the machine is ready.

Pre-infusion is passive via the saturated group — mild soak on shot start. Not programmable like the Ride's active mode, not adjustable like the Breville's pressure-programmable pre-infusion. Simpler.

## Grinder pairing

Specialita is a good match. The Move is engineered for espresso-forward workflows; precise grinders matter. No modifications to the grinder side needed.

## Complexity and learning curve

Low. OLED interface is newer-generation Profitec; more readable than the older units. Saturated group demands less ritual than E61 (no cooling flush ever, period). Shot timer + volumetrics mean the machine tracks more variables automatically.

## Modification and upgrade potential

Small. The saturated group limits E61-specific mods:

- **4-hole steam tip** — faster milk, available from Profitec and retailers
- **Longer steam wand option**
- **Modular portafilter spouts**
- **No factory flow control** — and no known aftermarket flow control for this saturated group
- **No rotary pump conversion**

If flow control matters to you, do not buy the Move. Buy the Ride ($2,599) or go direct to a Bianca.

## Pros and cons

**Pros**
- **Lowest-price dual boiler** in Profitec's lineup at $1,999
- Saturated group: excellent brew temp stability without E61 complexity
- Dual independent PID with OLED display, shot timer, volumetrics
- Compact footprint (10.6 in W, 14.8 in H), 41 lb
- Fast warmup (9 min fast mode)
- Scheduled on/off programming
- Quiet vibratory pump operation

**Cons**
- **No E61 flow control kit compatibility** — the saturated group design has no known aftermarket flow control path
- Smaller 0.75 L steam boiler — slower milk vs full-size DB (Pro 600/Ride/Synchronika)
- No passive E61 pre-infusion mechanism (relies on simpler saturated-group pre-infusion)
- Modular portafilter spouts are proprietary (silicone inserts)
- Reservoir-only; not plumbable
- Newer product; fewer long-term owner reports than Pro 300 accumulated

## Who it's for

Someone who wants a DB in the $2,000 range with current-year build quality and OLED features. Also: anyone upgrading from a single boiler who wants a simpler workflow than E61 — the saturated group is "pull shot, move on."

**Not** for you if you want flow control (go Ride, Bianca, or Decent). Not for you if you make more than 2 milk drinks per session (the 0.75 L steam boiler is limiting; step to Pro 600/Ride).

For an even milk/espresso user: the Move is a strong pick at $1,999, directly competitive with the Lelit Elizabeth ($1,799) which uses a similar architecture (saturated group dual boiler) at $200 less. **Elizabeth still wins on feature count per dollar** (programmable pre-infusion via LCC, three shot buttons, paddle-style LCC deep programmability), but the Move has newer interface and Profitec's slightly more premium build feel. Direct head-to-head pick, both excellent.
