## Specs

- **Boiler:** 1.8 L brass with T.E.A. surface treatment (single HX, not dual boiler)
- **Pump:** Ulka vibratory with pulsor noise damping
- **Group:** E61 with automatic mechanical pre-infusion
- **Reservoir:** 3.0 L bottom-fed with dual float switches (unusual design — no hoses to the tank)
- **Wattage:** 1400 W
- **Voltage:** 110 V US confirmed
- **Build:** #304 stainless steel food-grade throughout; commercial E61 group

## Key features

The Andreja Premium has been one of the "reference HX" machines at its price point for 15+ years — the Evo designation indicates the post-2015 refreshed internals and aesthetics.

Distinguishing features:

- **1.8 L brass HX boiler** — large, insulated, generous steam
- **Bottom-fed reservoir with dual float switches** — unique design, no pickup hose to manage or kink
- **E61 mechanical pre-infusion** (standard)
- **Swivel no-burn wand** — excellent ergonomics for latte art pitchers
- **Optional PID** — US models typically ship with a pressurestat; PID variant adds ~$200-300 and brings per-degree adjustment + shot timer
- **Plumb-in pathway via aftermarket kits** — not factory standard but well-documented owner mod

What it isn't: stylish. The Andreja looks like a commercial machine miniaturized, not a design object. Owners who value utilitarian feel over polished aesthetic consistently rate the Andreja favorably.

## Steam and milk workflow

1.8 L HX boiler is among the largest in this wiki. Continuous steam, fast milk recovery between drinks. Home-Barista's steaming benchmark test gave the Andreja Premium a cappuccino score of 9.0 — tied for best HX on their testbed.

The swivel wand is a real ergonomic win — rotate and pitch the wand to fit any pitcher size. 2-hole stock tip; upgradeable.

Simultaneous brew and steam, as expected.

## Brew workflow and temperature stability

HX workflow means cooling flush after idle (standard 3-5 second purge) before pulling a shot. With pressurestat stock, temperature variance is typical HX ±1-2 °C depending on flush discipline. PID variant drops variance meaningfully.

E61 mechanical pre-infusion is stock — forgiving, good for distribution errors.

## Grinder pairing

Specialita is well-matched. The Andreja is a forgiving machine that rewards any consistent grinder; no special pairing requirements.

## Complexity and learning curve

Low to moderate. Cooling flush ritual is the main HX-specific skill. Steaming is straightforward. Owners frequently cite the Andreja as "low-drama" — it just works.

## Modification and upgrade potential

Strong:

- **E61 flow control kit** — direct compatibility with Quick Mill or aftermarket kits
- **PID retrofit** — if bought with pressurestat, aftermarket PID installs are well-documented on Home-Barista
- **Steam tip upgrades** — 3-hole, 4-hole options
- **Direct plumb kit** — aftermarket, requires DIY comfort
- **Parts availability** — excellent through Chris' Coffee, 1st-line.com, Coffeeionado

One user on Home-Barista reports 6+ years of ownership with only a vibe pump replacement — typical reliability signal.

## Pros and cons

**Pros**
- **1.8 L HX boiler** — among the largest steam capacities on this list
- Swivel no-burn wand — excellent ergonomics
- Bottom-fed 3.0 L reservoir — clever, no hose clutter
- Proven reliability (long production run, deep Home-Barista history)
- E61 flow control kit compatible
- 2-year Chris' Coffee warranty
- No-gimmicks utilitarian feel

**Cons**
- **16.0 in height** — right at the clearance limit; 0.5-1 in cup rail can exceed most under-cabinet constraints. **Measure before buying.**
- **PID is optional, not stock on US variants** — expect $2,050-2,150 for a PID-equipped configuration
- Less modern aesthetics vs Profitec/ECM/Lelit (more industrial utilitarian)
- Smaller US brand presence; fewer YouTube reviews
- Reservoir-only stock (direct plumb is aftermarket)
- Steam wand valve ergonomics are utilitarian (not joystick)

## Who it's for

Someone who wants a commercial-grade HX with no-nonsense build, values large steam capacity, and doesn't care about marketing polish or modern UI. Also: someone with a comfortable under-cabinet clearance (17 in+) — the 16-inch height is tight.

**Not** for you if under-cabinet clearance is strictly 16 in or less. Not for you if you want a stock PID (the PID variant adds cost and is often out of stock). Not for you if you want current-generation aesthetics — the Andreja looks dated next to a Ride or Synchronika.

For an even milk/espresso user, the Andreja is a solid HX pick at $1,850, genuinely competitive with the Mara X ($1,699) and Pro 400 ($1,699). The Andreja's 1.8 L steam boiler is the biggest on the HX list. **Verify the 16-inch clearance before committing.**
